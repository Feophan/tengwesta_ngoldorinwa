Quantifiers

kilY

The quantifier kilY in the singular means each, every. In the plural, it means all:

kilY ko/o  each/every city (sometimes: an entire city)

kilYe ko/o`  all the cities

The corresponding universal pronouns are kil~qen~ everyone and kil_i all.

qn

The quantifier qn means whole, entire:

tirj qn tm  Look at the entire matter.

ke`

ke` means by itself, alone, the only:

ke` hinYn n k[ kiryke  To my only child shall be given a crown.

ke[  and  xYn

The basic meaning of ke[ is other (out of many), stressing similarity. The basic meaning of xYn is other (than something else), stressing difference:

jolm` k` ke[e ljm`  consonants and other sounds;

jolm` xYne ljm` lj jom`  connsonants are different sounds than vowels.

Indefinite Marker as Quantifier

In many cases the indefinite marker (→4.7) can be translated to English with quantifier some. When it stands with the article it means many:

kifl~mlinN` kibe met~  [LotR/377]. Upon the many foaming waves between us two.

When modified by a quantifier njotim it means (a) few:

cenNen njotime ke#li  [PE22/155]. I saw a few Elves.
