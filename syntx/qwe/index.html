Questions

Introduction: Basic Terminology

There are two main types of questions:

  • Questions that are answered by yes or no: ‘yes/no-questions’ – cf. Are whales fish? A subset of this type consists of questions that give two (or more) alternatives to choose from: ‘alternative questions’ or ‘double questions’ – cf. Are whales fish or mammals?
  • Questions that are answered by specifying one or more persons, things, places, etc. (or ‘no one’, ‘nothing’, etc.). Such questions are commonly known as ‘wh-questions’ (as they are, in English, usually introduced by a ‘wh-word’, such as who?, when?, why? etc.), or as ‘specifying questions’: cf. What kind of animal are whales?

For indirect/dependent questions, i.e. the representation of questions in indirect speech/thought (cf. Engl. He asked what kind of animal whales are), →@@.
